working papers

Four papers. Every number reproducible.

Each macro model ships with a working paper setting out its equations, its implementation, and — most importantly — how far it can be checked against ground truth. Read them here in the browser, or download the PDF. Sources and figure-reproduction scripts live in the papers/ directory of the repo.

01 — the papers

Ours against the official model.

Three of the four papers are replications with a published anchor, so the headline result is a distance: how far our implementation sits from the institution's own. The fourth has no anchor to sit against, and says so.

obr-macro · UK · hosted

An open emulator of the OBR macroeconometric model

Transpiles the OBR's 372 published EViews equations into Python, solves them by Gauss–Seidel iteration, and anchors the baseline to the November 2025 Economic and Fiscal Outlook.

Anchored to the Nov 2025 EFO: GDP MAPE 0.18%, consumption 0.29% over 2025Q1–2027Q4 — hard-gated in CI. Free-running fit reported without flattery: only 4 of 11 computed variables in band. read in browser · 35 pages → boe-svar · UK · hosted

Replicating the Bank of England's structural VAR

An eight-variable Bayesian SVAR with a Minnesota prior and zero plus sign restrictions imposed via Arias et al. (2018), identifying six named structural shocks.

Out-of-sample from the 2024Q2 edge: RMSE 0.32pp for both GDP growth and CPI inflation, with 14/14 outturns inside the 68% bands. Global shocks explain 40.9% of GDP and 50.1% of CPI FEV at one year, vs ~40% and ~50% in the paper. read in browser · 28 pages → frb-us · US · local

A modern Python implementation of FRB/US

A from-scratch reimplementation of the Fed's 284-equation FRB/US model under VAR expectations, with a sparse analytic Jacobian and a damped Newton solver.

Baseline reproduces LONGBASE to 5.6e-17 (machine precision); an identical 100bp funds-rate shock matches the Fed's pyfrbus to 1.4e-8 against current pyfrbus 1.1.1 (6.0e-9 vs 1.0.0) across all 284 variables and 20 quarters. read in browser · 36 pages → psl-og · UK · US · local

The PSL overlapping-generations model in PolicyEngine Macro

OG-Core deployed through its OG-UK calibration, with 80 age cohorts and effective- and marginal-tax-rate functions estimated from PolicyEngine microdata.

No published ground truth — unlike the OBR and SVAR members this is a structural counterfactual, calibrated to ONS and OBR national-accounts targets rather than validated against a replication anchor. read in browser · 34 pages →
